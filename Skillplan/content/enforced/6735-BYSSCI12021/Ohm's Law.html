Learn

In a circuit, voltage, current and resistance are related to each other. Current is directly proportional to voltage. This means that higher voltage causes a greater flow of electrons, or a greater current.

On the other hand, current is inversely proportional to resistance. High resistance causes low current flow because it's harder for the voltage to push electrons through the resistor.

Ohm's Law

 Ohm's law is a set of formulas used to describe the relationship between voltage, current and resistance.

E = I x R

Ohm's Law states: it takes 1 volt to push 1 amp through 1 ohm of resistance.
