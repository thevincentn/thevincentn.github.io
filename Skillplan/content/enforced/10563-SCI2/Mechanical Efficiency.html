Introduction

So far, you’ve seen how machines like levers, pulleys and gears help us do work. In each case, we assumed an “ideal” machine with no energy losses but that’s not how the real world works.

Every time we use a machine, some of the input energy is "lost" because materials can flex or deform and there could friction or head loss. This energy isn't destroyed but instead just becomes unusable. That means the actual mechanical advantage (AMA) is usually less than the ideal mechanical advantage (IMA).

Correct Answer: Work In > Work Out

In an ideal system, Work In = Work Out. But since energy is typically lost in the system, Work In is greater than Work Out.

Efficiency

Efficiency is a way to measure how much of the input work a machine turns into useful output work. It's expressed as a percentage:

Efficiency (%) = Actual Effort / Expected Effort = Actual Mechanical Advantage (AMA) / Ideal Mechanical Advantage (IMA)

If a machine puts in 100N of effort to lift a 98 N load, the machine is 98% efficient.

Note

Note: A machine’s efficiency can never be greater than 1 (100%). If you are not sure which number is the AMA and which is the IMA, simply divide the smaller number by the larger number.
