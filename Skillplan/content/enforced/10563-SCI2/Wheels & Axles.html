Multiplying Torque

A wheel and axle is a simple machine consisting of a larger circular object attached to a smaller cylindrical object. When torque is applied to the system to rotate one, the other turns too.

Torque depends on how much force is applied and how far from the center it is applied. Turning a large wheel takes less force than turning a small axle, but the force must be applied over a longer distance. This is how the wheel and axle creates mechanical advantage, allowing us to lift heavier loads or move objects more easily by trading force for distance. Types of Wheel & Axle Systems

There are three types of wheel and axle systems.

Wheel Drives AxleAxle Drives WheelCompound Systems

In this setup, the effort is applied to the larger wheel, which turns the axle. This multiplies the torque delivered to the axle, making it easier to turn something with resistance. These systems provide a mechanical advantage by allowing a smaller input force to create a larger output force.

Common examples include steering wheels, screwdrivers and wrenches. These tools let you apply a small effort over a large distance to do more work.

You’ll find this system in bicycle pedals and motor shafts where a small input rotates a much larger wheel to increase travel distance or rotational speed.

Compound systems use multiple wheels and axles connected together to either increase torque or control speed. These systems are often found in machinery, hand cranks and lifting devices where one set of wheels drives another.

Each stage in the system can add more mechanical advantage or fine-tune movement making it easier to lift heavy loads or apply controlled force.

Equivalent Advantage

Whether you’re increasing force, speed or distance, the same formula applies. Mechanical advantage works the same way for torque as it does for motion.

Calculating Mechanical Advantage

You can calculate the mechanical advantage of the wheel and axle using the radius of the wheel (RW) and the radius of the axle (RA).

MA = RW ÷ RA

The mechanical advantage of a wheel with a radius of 20 inches and an axle with a radius of 5 inches is 4.

Problem 1: What does the radius of an axle need to be for a wheel and axle system that has a mechanical advantage of 2 and a wheel radius of 3.75 ft?

Step 1: Identify

  • Know: mechanical advantage = 2, radius of the wheel = 3.75ft

Step 2: Organize

Find the formula that relates the radius of the axle (RA) to the radius of the wheel (RW) and mechanical advantage (MA).

RA = RF ÷ MA

Step 3: Set Up & Calculate

Substitute the values:

RA = 3.75 ÷ 2 = 1.875

Step 4: Decide

Does the answer make sense?

Yes! The radius of the axle needs to be smaller than the wheel and 1.875 is smaller than 3.75. ✅

Calculating Speed & Distance

Mechanical advantage can also be calculated using the linear speed of the wheel (LW) and the linear speed of the axle (LA).

MA = LW ÷ LA

If an axle can travel at 2m/s in a system with a mechanical advantage of 3, the wheel will travel at 6m/s.

To understand where linear speed comes from, imagine unwrapping the circumference of a circle into a straight line. If the circle rotates once per second, we'll travel 1 circumference in that second.

Since distance is speed multiplied by time, we can calculate how far we can travel with a wheel and axle using the following formula.

d = LW ÷ t

If the linear speed of a wheel is 6m/s, we can travel 18m in 3 seconds.

Problem 1: A truck's wheel and axle system has a mechanical advantage of 4, and the axle is spinning with a linear speed of 2.5 m/s. How far can the vehicle travel in 5 seconds?

Open All Panels

Step 1: Identify

  • Need: distance and linear speed of wheel
  • Know: mechanical advantage = 4, linear speed of the axle = 2.5m/s and time = 5 s

Step 2: Organize

Find the formula that relates the linear speed of the wheel (LW) to the mechanical advantage (MA) and the linear speed of the axle (LA).

LW = MA × LA

Find the formula that relates distance (d) to speed (LW) and time (t).

d = LW × t

Step 3: Set Up & Calculate

Substitute the values:

LW = 4 × 2.5 = 10m/s

LW = 10m/s

d = 10 × 5 = 50m

Step 4: Decide

Does the answer make sense?

Yes! The distance travelled is reasonable. ✅

Final Answer: The vehicle will travel 50m in 5s.
