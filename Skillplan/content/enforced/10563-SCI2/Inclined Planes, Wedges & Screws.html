Introduction

There are three types of inclined planes.

Click on each to learn more.

RampsWedgesScrews

A ramp is a flat, slanted surface that connects a lower point to a higher point. Ramps make it easier to lift or move loads vertically by spreading the force needed over a longer distance. The gentler the slope, the less force required.

A wedge is a moving inclined plane. Instead of helping to lift something up, a wedge uses force to push two objects apart or to split a single object. Think axes, knives and chisels. The mechanical advantage comes from converting a forward motion into a force that acts outward on the material.

A screw is essentially an inclined plane wrapped around a cylinder. Turning the screw applies a small rotational force over a long inclined path, allowing a much larger force to be exerted vertically. Screws are often used to hold materials together or to lift loads.

Ramps Are Most Common

It is not typical for tradespeople to do mechanical advantage calculations for wedges or screws.

An inclined plane requires a small effort (IF) over a longer, slanted distance (ED) to overcome the larger force (OF) it would take to lift the load straight up over a shorter vertical distance (RD). You can explore this relationship in the activity below.

Calculating Mechanical Advantage

Calculating the mechanical advantage of a force multiplying tool can be confusing with so many interchangeable terms. Take a moment to familiarize yourself with how the terms below are related to one another.

Just like levers, you can calculate the mechanical advantage of an inclined plane using the resistance force (RF) and effort force (EF).

MA = RF ÷ EF

The mechanical advantage of a ramp that can lift a 100lb load with 10lbs of effort is 10.

Using Lengths

Mechanical advantage for inclined planes can also be calculated using the slant length / resistance distance (RD) and the vertical rise / effort distance (ED).

MA = RD ÷ ED

The mechanical advantage of an inclined plane that has a slant length of 8ft and a vertical rise of 2ft is 4.
