Introduction

Belt drives are a special type of pulley and belt system that connect an electric motor to another part of the machine. The pulley connected to the motor is called the driver pulley or motor pulley and the pulley connected to the machine is called the driven pulley. Sometimes these pulleys are called shafts, rotors or wheels.

The mechanical advantage for belt drives is primarily based on the ratio of the pulley diameters. You can explore this relationship in the activity below.

The Moment of Force Equation

With belt drives we aren't interested in the mechanical advantage we gain from force. Instead, we're interested in the speed advantage we get from the motor pulley. But we can still use the moment of force equation to find out things about our belt drive system.

EF × ED = RF × RD

Mechanical Advantage = Gear Ratio = ED / RD = RF / EF

Force = Rotations Per Minute (RPM)

Distance = Diameter or Radius

If the belt drive's motor pulley has a diameter of 50 inches and the driven pulley's diameter is 25 inches, then the mechanical advantage is 2.

Problem 1: A motor with a 225 mm diameter pulley drives a fan with a 75 mm diameter pulley. Calculate the mechanical advantage of the belt drive.

Open All Panels

Step 1: Identify

  • Need: mechanical advantage (MA)
  • Know: diameter of the motor pulley (ED) and the diameter of the driven pulley (RD)

Step 2: Organize

Find the formula that relates mechanical advantage to the diameter of the motor pulley (ED) and the diameter of the driven pulley (RD).

MA = ED / RD

Step 3: Set Up & Calculate

Substitute the values:

MA = 225 / 75 = 3

Step 4: Decide

Does the answer make sense?

Yes! The mechanical advantage is reasonable ✅.

Final Answer: The mechanical advantage is 3.


When a large motor pulley is used to drive a smaller pulley, there's an increase in the system's speed, just like with gears. Let's see how we can use the moment of force equation to find the speed of the driven pulley.


Problem 2: A motor operating at 1600 rpm has a pulley diameter of 3.5 inches. It drives a 9 inch pulley. Calculate the rpm of the driven gear.

Open All Panels

Step 1: Identify

Know:

  • diameter of the motor pulley (ED) = 3.5 inches
  • the diameter of the driven pulley (RD) = 9 inches
  • RPM of the motor pulley (EF) = 1600 RPM

Need:

  • RPM of the driven gear (RF)

Step 2: Organize

Find the formula that relates these known values.

EF × ED = RF × RD

Step 3: Set Up & Calculate

Substitute the values.

1600 × 3.5 = RF × 9

Solve for the missing variable.

Include the units.

RF = 1600 RPM × 3.5 inch / 9 inch

Calculate.

RF = 622.22 RPM

Step 4: Decide

Does the answer make sense?

Yes! The driven gear is bigger so it would move more slowly ✅.

Final Answer: The driven pulley rotates at 622.22 RPM.
