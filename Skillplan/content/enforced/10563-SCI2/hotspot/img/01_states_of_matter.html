States of Matter

Solids Liquids Gases

Solids

Characteristic

  • Definite Shape
  • Definite volume
  • Cannot be compressed

Examples

Concrete, glass, iron rods

Behaviour of Molecules

Molecules don’t move much. They are packed tightly together and bond with each other to make a stable object.

This is why solids have a definite shape and can’t be compressed.

Liquids

Characteristic

  • Take the shape of the container
  • Definite volume
  • Cannot be compressed

Examples

Water, oil, refrigerant liquids

Behaviour of Molecules

Molecules have more movement. They are close together but they are weakly bonded to each other. Molecules collide with each other and the surface of their container. This is why liquids take the shape of the container.

Gases

Characteristic

  • No definite Shape
  • No definite volume
  • Can be compressed

Examples

Concrete, glass, iron rods

Behaviour of Molecules

Molecules have lots of movement. They are not bonded to each other and their collisions cause them to move very far apart, if not contained. This is why gases expand indefinitely and can also be compressed.
