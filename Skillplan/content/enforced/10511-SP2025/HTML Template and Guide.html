True or False

Lessons typically start with one or two What Do You Think questions.

True

WDYT questions are our way of testing people's prior knowledge and to engage our participants from the get-go. They're typically easy enough to give the participant a quick win and are an opportunity to teach something potentially new.

True or False

Flip cards like these are the only way we ask What Do You Think questions.

False

While flip cards are typically the most common way we ask WDYT questions at the beginning of a lesson, they're not the only types of questions we use.

Connecting with our Participants

At the end of the day, we're developing these training materials for people and so we want to do our best to connect with them. Here are a few strategies you can use when developing your eLearning.

Click on the arrows to learn more.

Contextualize

How do we make a lesson on imperial and metric units engaging? By connecting it to the work our participants do every single day on the job site.

Tell a Story

Help participants visualize how their learning will applicable to their work by immersing them in a story or scenario.

Empathesize

Ask yourself questions like "Why would this matter to me if I were a ______?" or "These ideas are too complex. How can I make it easier to understand?" to make the training more relevant to the participant.

Meet Barbara

Barbara has been working as an heavy equipment operator her entire adult life. She is 53 years old now and is constantly complaining about feeling achey, sore, and tired. Barbara isn't sure she can continue to work much longer but hasn't been planning for retirement. She's primarily concerned about what her retirement plans look like.

Barbara appears in Respectful and Inclusive Workplaces and Managing your Money.

Meet Chelsea

Chelsea is a young lady currenting exploring opportunities in the trades. She's been invited to a few job sites that have had to locate special sizes of PPE to accomodate her smaller figure.

Chelsea appears on our lander and in Respectful and Inclusive Workplaces and Managing your Money.

Meet Hibah

Hibah appears in Supporting Learning Differences in the Trades.

Meet Jack

Meet Steven

Steven has had a financially successful career. He's bounced around industries chasing money his entire career. Because he was so well off he was able to borrow money for nice cars and even a mortgage for a house in an expensive city. His biggest hauls came from working in the Alberta oil sands until the markets crashed in 2010. When this happened, he found it very challenging to pay his debts to lenders and has been struggling since. Now he's working hard to pay his debts off and teachs others how to be more financially responsible.

Steven appearsin Respectful and Inclusive Workplaces, Preparing for the Red Seal Exam and Managing your Money.
