INDIGENOUS AWARENESS

POST-TRAINING TOOLS




Overview

Indigenous Awareness Training provides the knowledge and skills required to participate in reconciliation and contribute to supportive work environments for Indigenous tradespeople. The online course prepares workers and leaders to work effectively with Indigenous populations within the context of the building trades.

As part of the federally funded Industry Training Champions (ITC) Project, several tools have been developed to reinforce training goals and support a more meaningful and lasting impact of the Indigenous Awareness Training program.

About the Industry Training Champions (ITC) Project

The ITC Project is designed to maximize learning transfer and skill application in the workplace. It provides dual-focused training for both workers and leaders, integrating Skills for Success with the history of Indigenous Peoples and situations happening in the construction industry today.

What are Skills for Success?

The Government of Canada’s Skills for Success framework identifies nine skills that are critical to success in a rapidly changing world.

Indigenous Awareness Training targets four skills:

  • Communication
  • Collaboration
  • Adaptability
  • Problem Solving

These skills are developed and applied through practical workplace scenarios, equipping participants with career development tools to strengthen relationships with Indigenous coworkers, employees and communities.

Post-Training Reinforcement Tools

To reinforce the online course learning, there are three post-training tools:

  • Follow-up Email – A reminder of key takeaways and additional resources to support continued learning, sent automatically one to two weeks after course completion.
  • Toolbox Talks – Brief, on-the-job meetings to reinforce learning in a practical setting.
  • Debrief Session – A structured group discussion to reflect on key learnings from the online course and explore real-world applications, adaptable in length and content for organization-specific needs.

Purpose of Post-Training Reinforcement

Post-training reinforcement is critical for ensuring the transfer of learning, which refers to the ability of participants to apply the knowledge and skills gained during training to their everyday work. Without reinforcement, employees may struggle to integrate new concepts into their roles effectively. The post-training tools serve as a bridge between learning and practical application by:

  • Encouraging reflection on key concepts from the online course
  • Providing a space for discussion and sharing experiences
  • Helping participants identify practical ways to implement their learning
  • Strengthening commitment to reconciliation and a supportive workplace culture

Benefits to Your Organization

Participation in the ITC Project supports your organization in building a stronger, more inclusive workforce—at no cost. Benefits include:

  • Free access to high-quality online training (funded by the project) for both workers and leaders
  • Post-training tools (emails, toolbox talks, debrief sessions) to help participants apply what they’ve learned on the job
  • Improved social-emotional skills and intercultural competence for your team
  • Pre- and post-training surveys to measure impact and track skill development
  • Custom reports for organizations with 30+ participants, highlighting training outcomes
  • Progress toward meeting diversity, inclusion and Indigenous relations goals

To learn more about these opportunities, please contact Lee Lagan, Manager of Instructional Design, at llagan@skillplan.ca.

About this Resource

These short toolbox talks are designed to reinforce key messages from the Indigenous Awareness course your crew has already completed. They're designed for timely relevance and bring course concepts into real jobsite conversations.

Overview

Meeting Details:

  • All participants should have completed the online Indigenous Awareness course.
  • Each talk takes about 5 minutes.
  • Designed to be delivered by a jobsite leader as part of regular safety meetings.

Each talk includes a few discussion questions and key points to guide conversation. You don’t need to be an expert — just ask the questions, give people a chance to respond and use the talking points provided. These sessions are meant to be short, interactive and respectful.

By keeping these conversations going on-site, you’re helping to build a more informed, respectful and inclusive workplace.
