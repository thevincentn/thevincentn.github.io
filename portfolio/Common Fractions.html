Tips & Tricks

Memorizing them can help you easily convert between the fraction and decimal forms of these numbers. But this can be challenging, especially without a lot of on the job practice. To help you remember them, consider the following.

Decimal Places Easy References Odds Only

There is a pattern between a fraction’s denominator and the number of decimal places. Knowing it can help you quickly identify if you’re converting halves, 4ths, 8ths or 16ths. 

Examples
  1. The decimal for 1/2 has 1 decimal place: 0 . 5
  2. The decimal for 3/4 has 2 decimal place: 0 . 7 5
  3. The decimal for 5/8 has 3 decimal place: 0 . 6 2 5
  4. The decimal for 11/16 has 4 decimal place: 0 . 6 8 7 5

Did You Know?

The decimal form of all these common fractions end in 5.

2. Use reference numbers.
3. Convert.
