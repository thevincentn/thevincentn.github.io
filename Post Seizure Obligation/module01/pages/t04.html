Different Forms

Topic Learning Outcome:

  1. Distinguish between different Form 5.2s.
    1. Recognize where they are to be used.
    2. Identify where they can be accessed on the Vancouver Police Department Intranet website.

Section 489.1 of the Criminal Code (1985) requires the officer seizing property to file a Form 5.2 to the justice who authorized the search. This includes seized drugs for evidence or destruction and possible stolen property seized from any person. The seizing officer must ensure that they are using the appropriate and most up-to-date form. The Vancouver Police Department Regulations and Procedures Manual (VPD RPM) notes that there are five different Form 5.2s:

Click on each link to download a copy of each form 5.2s.

1.9.4 Seized Property

  1. Form 5.2 Report to a Justice (Following Seizure of Property And / Or Warrant to Search). This is the basic form used to report a seizure of property.
    • PCR 087 Form 5.2 for seizures conducted under Federal statutes.
    • PCR 815 Form 5.2 (electronic version only – available on the Intranet under Forms) for seizures conducted under Provincial statutes.
  2. PCR 094 - Attachment to Report to a Justice. This is a continuation of the Form 5.2, used to list additional property when the space for listing property on the bottom of the Form 5.2 is insufficient.
  3. PCR0888b Form 5.2 Report to a Justice (Following Warrant or Demand to Take Blood Samples). This form is used whenever a blood sample is taken pursuant to a warrant or demand for an impaired investigation.
  4. PCR141 Form 5.07 Report to a Provincial Court Judge of The Court for DNA Analysis. This report is required any time an officer obtains a DNA sample following a DNA warrant.

(VPD RPM, 2005)
