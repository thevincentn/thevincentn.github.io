Criminal Code (1985)

The Criminal Code (1985) requires judicial control over property seized by police officers while investigating any offence under the Criminal Code (1985) or other statute passed by the Parliament of Canada. Sections 489.1 to 490.1 of the Criminal Code (1985) provides a statutory framework for the detention, disposal, forfeiture and return of seized property. Whereby seized property refers to

Any property that comes into the possession of a member during the course of an investigation, and is not considered found property, relinquished property or property for safekeeping.

(VPD RPM, 2012)

Section 490

Officers who seize property under section 489 are obliged to safeguard the items they have seized (R v Strilec, 2010). The procedures officers must follow upon seizing property are governed under section 489.1. These procedures apply to property seized under a warrant, warrantless, or otherwise under other Acts of Parliament.

Procedures outlined under section 489.1 includes the filling of a Form 5.2 â€“ Report to a Justice. The purpose of this form is to place the property under judicial oversight. The form creates accountability and imposes a measure of protection upon the property (R v Canary, 2018) and will be discussed throughout this course.
