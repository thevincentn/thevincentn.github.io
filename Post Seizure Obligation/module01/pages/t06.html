Legal Ramifications

Topic Learning Outcome:

  1. List potential pitfalls of failing to file a Form 5.2 properly.

Failure to comply with the requirement of filing a Form 5.2 - Report to Justice may prevent property from being entered in court as evidence. Consider the following case study where failure to file appropriate warrants and forms (including Form 5.2) resulted in an acquittal of the murder of an innocent man.

Case Study
R v Gill (2021)

On April 27th, 2011, Mr. Banbir Kajla was rear ended while driving his newly wedded wife. The two had gotten married the morning of the accident. Mr. Kajla left his vehicle to exchange information with the driver of the vehicle when he was shot and killed. The shooter then approached Mr. Kajla’s vehicle and shot at the passenger side towards Mr. Kajla’s wife. Fortunately, he missed.

The Crown alleges that the shooter was Mr. Samandeep Gill. Gill was charged in May 2018 with second-degree murder and attempted murder. He was acquitted in March of 2021 as a result of the exclusion of evidence under section 24 of the Charter of Rights and Freedoms (1982). The ruling of the exclusion arose as the Surrey RCMP’s Integrated Homicide Investigation Team (IHIT) failed to appropriately file Form 5.2 and subsequent forms for extending the detention of seized property for evidence. Crown ruled that this failure breached Mr. Gill’s section 8 and 24 Charter rights resulting in the omission of evidence.

The tragic death of Mr. Kajla and the miscarriage of justice which followed is a reminder of being diligent with throughout the investigative process. This includes the filing of relevant forms such as Form 5.2.

The courts have stated that failure to file a 5.2 form does not itself render a valid search invalid (R v Arason, 1992). It does, however, render the detention of the items unreasonable and in breach of section 8 (R v Craig, 2016; R v Butters, 2014). Justice David M. Paciocco (R v Butters, 2014) notes that “failing to file a Report to a Justice relating to a warrantless search is unacceptable” and is a “serious breach”.

Recognizing the importance of filing Form 5.2s and relevant procedures will minimize these breaches and promote a fair judicial process. These procedures will be discussed in further detail in subsequent sections of the course.

Important

A Form 5.2 Report must be completed whenever officers seize anything, including drugs, for an investigative or legal process. This would include items seized under Federal or Provincial statutory authority or under common law powers.

(VPD RPM, 2005)
