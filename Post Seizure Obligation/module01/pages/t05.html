Common Errors

Topic Learning Outcome:

  1. Acknowledge common errors that can occur when filing a Form 5.2.
  2. Describe the circumstances in which the Property & Forensic Storage Services Office may refuse to accept property.

Filing a Form 5.2 - Report to Justice is considered the "gateway" to "important procedural protections under sections 489.1 and 490‚Äù and so must be done as soon as possible (R v Reeves, 2017; R v Garcia-Machado, 2015). The Vancouver Police Department Property & Forensic Storage Services Office (Property Office) has identified a widespread problem concerning the completion of 5.2s. Use the Correct Form

When completing the form, members shall ensure they have the most up-to-date version.

Name of the Submitting Officer

On the very top line of the Form 5.2 - Report to Justice, just under the heading, is a space for the name of the submitting officer. Some members are forgetting to fill in this line.

Warrantless Search Information

In Box #2, titled "If no warrant has been issued", members are not including all necessary information. Many members are searching for a person and seizing items. It is necessary to include both the person‚Äôs name and the location where the search took place i.e., including street address, and the city or municipality.

Disposition of the Property

In Box #3, each item seized must be listed, including its disposition. The Form 5.2 - Report to Justice uses "A" for items returned to the owner (and you must include the name of the person it was returned to). If the item is to be detained to be dealt with by law (either held for evidence or held for the court to determine ownership), then "B" is specified as well as the location where the item is detained. Members are not completing this portion of the form correctly, resulting in the Property Office being unable to process the seized property.

Signature of Submitting Officer

Directly under Box # 3 is a space for the date and location of the seizure and signature of the submitting officer. Some members are forgetting to sign the form. Officers should also include their PIN numbers and current assignment below the signature line so that these forms can be traced back to the appropriate member if there is a problem.

Application for Detention

The last box on the page is the application for detention. The submitting officer must complete this if property is to be detained for investigation or court purposes. Officers must ensure that they include the appropriate item numbers for detained property in this application i.e., only those items which have a "B" as a disposition code should be listed.

Disposition of Form 5.2

A photocopy of the Form 5.2 - Report to Justice shall be attached to the property when tagged at the Property Office.

The Property Office will exercise their right of refusal to accept property in the following circumstances:

  • Property tagged as evidence or for investigation where there is no copy of a properly and legibly completed 5.2 attached
  • Relinquished property where there is no copy of a signed authorization from the person relinquishing attached (copy of signed notebook entry would suffice)
  • Property tagged for destruction where there is no copy of a signed authorization from the person relinquishing attached (copy of signed notebook entry would suffice)
  • Property tagged where there is no GO in Prime completed
  • Property that in the opinion of the Property Office staff is unsafely or improperly tagged (i.e., unsafe knives or firearms, items without fully completed property tags, etc.)

A substantial number of these forms are being completed incorrectly or not at all. This, in turn, causes delays and problems for the Property Office and Crown, and may result in property being returned to the owner who is often the suspect or accused. Improperly completed forms are regularly sent back to members for proper completion.

Members who are returned incomplete forms must return the corrected forms as soon as possible to the Property Office. This ensures that the Property Office can forward it to the courts. The prompt return of the corrected forms will ensure that none of the seized property will be returned to the owner/accused/suspect before trial. Time is of the essence in completing these forms.
