Crime Scene 4: Requirements and General Guidelines

Learning Outcome(s):

  1. Text
  2. Text

Forensic photographs, even when in an order that demonstrates relevancy, are less effective if they are not properly exposed, out of focus, or do not follow other general guidelines.

Click on the icon to read the scenario for the crime scene below.

Scenario

Scenario

This scenario is a break and enter into a residential basement workshop. The complainant returned to their workspace and noticed that someone had broken in overnight through an unlocked window and stolen some tools. The surveillance video shows the suspect wearing gloves on entry but then dropped one on the floor inadvertently. The suspect also left a drink can behind. Some tools were missing and a glove and drink can were located on the scene. The complainant has subsequently closed and locked the window. The patrol members took photographs at the scene, seized the pop can and glove. The pop can was subsequently tagged for fingerprinting in a Kootenay Science Locker and DNA analysis was requested for both exhibits through National Forensic Laboratory Services.

Practice

Compare the images taken at this scene and choose the one that better meets image requirements and general guidelines for effective forensic photographs.

Look for:

  • Correct sequencing
  • Clear linkage
  • Proper exposure
  • Sharp focus
  • Framing of the subject
