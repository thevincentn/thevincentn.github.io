Admissibility of Photographs

Learning Outcome(s):

  1. Explain the criteria for the admissibility of photographs.

Photographs must meet, at a minimum the following five criteria to be considered admissible in court.

Canada Evidence Act (R.S.C., 1985, c. C-5)

When weighing probative value against prejudicial effect, consider the following:

Probative Value

The probative value of evidence is the extent to which it proves a fact or facts about a file. Consider what can be reasonably inferred from the evidence, the weight of the evidence on the outcome, and how reliable it seems to be.

Prejudicial Effect

Prejudicial effect is when evidence may negatively impact the fairness and integrity of the case. Consider when photography may be misleading, confusing, or distracting. An example is victim injury photography taken at the hospital; think of the difference between a victim in a hospital bed or at a neutral location. Will the hospital images make injuries seem more severe?

Admissibility of Digital Photographs

Digital images have an additional burden when it comes to admissibility. Digital photographs must meet the following requirements under the Canada Evidence Act (1985).

Click on each to learn more.

Any person seeking to admit an electronic document has the burden of proving its authenticity. (s. 31(1))

For best evidence to be satisfied, there is a burden of proving the integrity of the electronic documents systems. (s. 31(2))

The process used to handle the digital images from capture to storage must also meet the standard for admissibility

Proper use of the Axon and Evidence.com system will ensure that your images will meet the digital admissibility requirement. Straying outside of the guidelines will mean there will be increase burden to prove their admissibility.

Any combination of taking photographs on a personal cell phone, uploading them to whichever cloud drive they use, emailing them to their work email, then changing the file type to upload them to versadex prior to deleting the images will not meet the evidentiary standard.

The remainder of this module is intent on teaching you how to take photographs in a manner that will increase their likelihood of admissibility.
