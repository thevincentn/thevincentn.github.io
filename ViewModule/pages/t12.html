Learning Outcome(s):

  1. Evaluate overall photographs for admissibility and effectiveness.
  2. Discuss practices to address situational challenges associated with overall forensic photographs.
    1. Evaluate forensic photographs for effective linkage.
    2. Explain how to provide linkage through crime scene photographs.
    3. Identify proper sequencing of forensic photographs.
    4. Evaluate midrange and close up photographs for admissibility and effectiveness.

Scenario

The following section will walk you through a series of forensic photographs taken at a crime scene, and will focus on the relevancy of the images. This scene involves theft from a room where the door was left open; therefore, no tool marks on the door. A surveillance video indicates that the suspect was wearing gloves during entry and while they were in the room. The presence of gloves suggests that no fingerprints will be available for processing. The video also shows the suspect leaving behind a can and a hat. These items are still present in the room.

These images, alongside notes and reports, will provide an accurate and unbiased record of the scene. Capturing images of a scene in a consistent and systematic order every time will ensure that those images meet requirements, and that they will support future testimony.

Relevancy is one requirement for effective forensic photographs; they also must be in focus and properly exposed. These aspects ensure that the scenes and subjects being captured are adequately visible in the images, and that they look like they would to the human eye.

Header Card

The first image must always be the completed header card. Note that this image meets requirements because:

  • it is the first image
  • it is filled out completely and legibly
  • the image is in focus and properly exposed
  • the card fills the frame

Overall

The final overall photograph is included to show parts of the room that may have been somewhat hidden in previous images, and is linked by the large whiteboard sitting on the floor. Always consider clutter like the stack of boxes and items of furniture that may block parts of the room.
