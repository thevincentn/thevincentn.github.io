Practice

Appropriately exposing images ensures that all important details are documented. Under exposed images (dark) reduces the vibrancy of colours making the photo inaccurate and hence obsolute. Over exposed images (bright) can make bright objects blend in with their surroundings e.g., clouds in the sky. Properly exposed images fit perfectly between both ends of this spectrum to provide just enough light to capture as much detail as possible.
