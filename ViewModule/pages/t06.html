Patrol: Responsibilities and Limits

Learning Outcome(s):

  1. Describe crime scenes and types of evidence appropriate for digital photography by Patrol members.
    1. Acknowledge situations that require consultation with the Forensic Identification Unit.
    2. Acknowledge situations that require photography services provided by the Forensic Identification Unit.

Many situations that call for photography will require consultation or services provided by the Forensic Identification Unit (FIU).

Click on the traffic lights below to learn more.

Red Light

Do Not Photograph
  • Weapon used in an offence
  • Any evidence related to: Murder, Suspicious Death, Assault, Robbery
  • Victim Injury
  • Search Warrants
  • Any items from existing RPM s section 1.6 – 1.8

Yellow Light

Consult with FIU
  • Time sensitive evidence Prior to FIU arrival *must call and consult FIU prior to taking a photo
  • Theft from Auto (TFA) *call FIU first
  • Break and Enter (BNE) *call FIU first
  • Motor Vehicle Incident (MVI) *call CIU first
  • Reportable Injury (not to be taken by injured member or witnessing member) *call FIU first

Green Light

Okay to Photograph
  • Property Crime: shoplifter, small theft, small theft from auto, seized property (street vending, seized weapon, etc)
  • Image of drugs tagged for Health Canada (for court reference)
  • Minor Victim Injury (outside FIU hours)
  • CCTV screen shot of suspect; Is it exigent? Is there risk of harm to public or police? Is image clear enough? If not exigent, consider FVU for still images
  • Image of suspect or person in custody (roadside) for identification and/or personal notebook reference

Best Practice

If during daytime hours, call FIU or the appropriate section to discuss and questions or uncertainties you may have. After hours, call your supervisor if you have any questions.
