Course Rationale

Photographs are often a crucial investigative aid, assisting the trier of fact later on in the courts. Photographs also serve as notes for the photographer. Like any other notes, they may be used to refresh one’s memory when describing the scene later in court. When done correctly, photographs provide better and more accurate documentation than human memory.

However, photographs do not automatically become court exhibits. To ensure that the photographs will serve these purposes, forensic photography should be done following an established set of guidelines. These guidelines have evolved over the years, and following them and understanding the reasons behind them is extremely important. This course will provide officers with the fundamental guidelines of forensic photography, and provide instructions around how to meet those guidelines using a Vancouver Police Department (VPD) issued mobile phone and the Axon software application.
