Minor Injury Photography: Purpose and Scope of Duties

Learning Outcome(s):

  1. Identify the purpose of injury photography.
  2. Explain the types of injury photography that are appropriate for Patrol members.

The purpose of police members taking injury photographs is to have accurate documentation of the injuries, taken by an unbiased third party. The photographs can assist the victim or the physician to describe the injury later in court. These images must meet the same requirements as scene or exhibit photographs, with the additional burden of considering the dignity and privacy of the individual.

Comfort and Dignity of the Subject

Ensuring accurate and unbiased documentation is always the goal of forensic photography; when taking injury photographs the comfort and dignity of the subject are also paramount. Remember that the subject of these photographs may have experienced a traumatizing event and that injuries may be in sensitive locations. This section will outline steps that can help to maintain the dignity of the subject while obtaining the necessary images. When in doubt, contact the Forensic Identification Unit for consultation or to take the photographs.

Female Subject

If the subject is female do your best to arrange for a female member to take the photographs.

Explain the Process

Consider the subject’s comfort while you are taking photos. Explain to them what you will be doing before and during the process.

Physical Ability

Consider their ability to stand throughout the process and where their injuries are located. Allow subject to sit for the photographs as needed.

Need to Disrobe

If the injury is in a sensitive location and/or requires the subject to disrobe, provide them with a plastic gown. Have them remove clothing items as required ensuring that they have privacy to appropriately do so. Instruct them to cover themselves with the plastic gown, and that you will have them expose only the injury or injuries when you are ready to photograph them.

Injuries frequently occur in sensitive areas or parts of the body that are obscured by clothing. Like scene photography, considering what images you will need to capture in advance and making a plan before starting can ensure you capture all the photographs you need and also maintain the dignity of the subject. Determining how much they will need to disrobe, providing them with cladding and privacy and discussing the plan with the subject can help to make everything go more smoothly.
